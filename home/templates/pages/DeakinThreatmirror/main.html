The Deakin Threat Mirror project is an open-source threat intelligence platform designed to aggregate, process, and visualize diverse threat feeds into actionable intelligence. It focuses on supporting small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) and developing economies by making cybersecurity more accessible and effective.

The primary goals are to revolutionize the way cybersecurity threats are analyzed and understood, to provide a cost-effective and efficient threat intelligence solution for SMEs and developing economies and to transform raw threat feeds and malware analysis patterns into actionable threat intelligence using advanced visual analytics, machine learning, and user-friendly interfaces.

The project addresses the critical challenge of consolidating and interpreting vast amounts of cyber threat data for SMEs and developing economies, which often lack the resources for commercial security tools. It aims to bridge this gap by offering enhanced protection against cyber-attacks.

In the long term, the project aims to create a lightweight, easy-to-use threat intelligence platform. This platform will transform raw threat feeds and malware analysis patterns into actionable threat intelligence using statistical data analysis, artificial intelligence, and machine learning, particularly benefiting SMEs and developing economies.

The project will provide SMEs and developing economies with a cost-effective and efficient threat intelligence solution. By making cybersecurity more accessible and effective, it will help these organizations protect themselves against cyber-attacks, despite their limited resources.

The project will provide small businesses with practical, hands-on training in cybersecurity. By engaging with realistic scenarios in a VR environment, users will gain a deeper understanding of how to protect their businesses against cyber threats, ultimately enhancing their overall cybersecurity posture.

The project leverages advanced visual analytics, machine learning, and user-friendly interfaces to transform raw data into insightful, actionable information. These technologies help in making cybersecurity more accessible and effective for organizations with limited resources.

Interested parties can get involved or contribute to the project by participating in the technical review, providing feedback, or contributing to the development of the platform. They can also follow the project’s progress and updates on platforms like GitHub
