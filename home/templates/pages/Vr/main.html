The Deakin CyberSafe VR project aims to revolutionize cybersecurity training for small businesses by leveraging interactive virtual reality (VR) technology. It focuses on bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application of cybersecurity measures among small business owners and employees.

The primary goals are to develop an immersive VR platform that simulates real-world cybersecurity scenarios, enhance the practical understanding of cybersecurity principles among small businesses and cover fundamental topics such as password security, data encryption, network security, safe web browsing, and recognizing common cyber threats like phishing and smishing.

The VR modules will cover essential cybersecurity topics, including password security, data encryption, Wi-Fi and network security, Safe web browsing and Common cyber-attacks like phishing and smishing

Users will navigate through virtual office spaces where they will identify and respond to cybersecurity threats. For example, they might identify and respond to phishing emails or infected USB drives, learn to identify suspicious emails and remove viruses, implement accounts with the least privileges, encrypt backup hard drives, configure device or Wi-Fi network security settings and use VPNs and install anti-virus software.

The project aims to create interactive VR modules tailored to the needs of small businesses and build a functional MVP (Minimum Viable Product) that demonstrates the core features of the Deakin CyberSafe VR platform.

The project will provide small businesses with practical, hands-on training in cybersecurity. By engaging with realistic scenarios in a VR environment, users will gain a deeper understanding of how to protect their businesses against cyber threats, ultimately enhancing their overall cybersecurity posture.

The Deakin CyberSafe VR project combines cutting-edge VR technology with comprehensive cybersecurity training. It offers an immersive and interactive learning experience that goes beyond traditional training methods, making it easier for users to apply cybersecurity principles in real-world situations.

Future plans include continuously testing and iterating on the VR modules to enhance their effectiveness. The project aims to expand its reach and impact by incorporating feedback from users and staying updated with the latest cybersecurity threats and best practices.
