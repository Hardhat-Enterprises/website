Frequently Asked Questions

Explore the answers to common questions about Smishing Detection

Smishing is a form of phishing attack executed via SMS (Short Message Service). Attackers send malicious messages to trick recipients into revealing sensitive information such as login credentials, financial details, or personal data.

Attackers often use leaked personal data to craft convincing messages that appear legitimate. These messages may contain malicious URLs or phone numbers, enticing recipients to click on links or call numbers, leading to data breaches or financial losses.

The project aims to develop AI-driven models to detect and identify smishing attempts in real-time. It includes creating a mobile application that integrates with smartphones to flag potential scam or spam messages, allowing users to decide whether to discard or ignore them.

AI-driven models, particularly those using machine learning and natural language processing, can analyze patterns and characteristics of SMS messages to determine their legitimacy. These models can identify subtle indicators of smishing attempts that may not be obvious to human users.

The mobile application integrates with smartphones to monitor incoming SMS messages. It uses the AI-driven models to analyze each message in real-time, flagging potential scam or spam messages. Users are then notified and can choose to delete or ignore the flagged messages.

The project combines comprehensive research, advanced AI techniques, and a user-friendly mobile application to provide a robust defense against smishing. It empowers users with real-time detection and decision-making capabilities, enhancing their security against SMS-based threats.

Users benefit by having an additional layer of security against smishing attacks. The application helps them identify and avoid potential scams, protecting their sensitive information and reducing the risk of financial losses.
